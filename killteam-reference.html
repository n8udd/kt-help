Battle Setup

Do these steps in order before the game starts. Both players do each step together unless stated otherwise.

Prepare the Battlefield

  • Both players agree on which Kill Teams they are using for this battle.
  • Both players together set up the terrain on the board to create the battlefield (called the Killzone).
  • Both players together place the objective markers â€” the tokens on the board that both sides will be fighting to control.

Decide Who Goes First

  • Both players each roll a dice. The player who rolls highest wins â€” this is called winning Initiative. Re-roll any ties.
  • The player who won the roll chooses which side of the board they want to deploy on (called the drop zone).
  • The player who lost the roll receives a re-roll card, which lets them force a re-roll of the Initiative dice at the start of a later round.

Choose Your Operatives â€” Secretly

  • Each player privately decides which individual fighters (operatives) from their Kill Team they are bringing to this battle.
  • The player who won Initiative reveals one of their chosen operatives first, then the other player reveals one of theirs. Continue alternating until all operatives have been revealed.

Choose Your Equipment â€” Secretly

  • Each player privately picks up to 4 equipment items from their Kill Team's equipment list. You can only take each type of equipment once â€” no duplicates.
  • The player who won Initiative reveals one equipment item first, then the other player reveals one of theirs. Continue alternating until all equipment has been revealed.

Receive Starting Command Points

  • Both players receive 2 Command Points (CP) each. CP are a currency you spend during the game â€” for example, to re-roll a single dice. Track yours with tokens or a notepad.

Choose Your Secret Bonus Objective (Tac Op)

  • Each player privately picks one Tactical Objective (Tac Op) from the list tied to their Kill Team's style of play (archetype). Don't reveal it yet.
  • This is an extra way to score Victory Points on top of the main board objectives. Completing it is a pleasant surprise for your opponent.

Place Your Operatives on the Board

  • First, both players assign equipment to their individual operatives one item at a time, alternating between players.
  • Then place operatives on the board. The player who won Initiative places one operative first, then the other player places one, and you alternate.
  • Place roughly one third of your team per round of placement (round fractions up). So if you have 10 operatives, you place 4, then 4, then 2.
  • Every operative starts the game in Conceal Order â€” they are lying low and cannot be shot at while they are in cover.

Begin the Game â€” Turning Point 1

  • The game is played over 4 Turning Points (think of them as rounds).
  • Right now, each player secretly picks their Primary Objective. This is your main scoring goal for the game and doubles as your Strategic Gambit (a declaration of intent) for this first Turning Point. Keep it hidden until the rules say to reveal it.

End of Battle â€” Who Wins?

  • After all 4 Turning Points are complete, both players count up their Victory Points. You earn these throughout the game by controlling objective markers, completing your Tac Op, and other in-game achievements.
  • The player with the most Victory Points wins. In the event of a tie, the player who scored more points in the final Turning Point wins.

Terrain

The battlefield is full of terrain pieces. Each type works differently â€” understanding them helps you position your operatives to survive longer and shoot more effectively.

Heavy Terrain Cover + Obscure

Big solid objects like walls, wrecked vehicles, and large crates. They provide cover (extra saves when shot at) and can also make your operative Obscured (attacker loses a hit and can't use Crits). Only one piece of terrain can provide the Obscured benefit per attack â€” you can't stack it.

Light Terrain Cover Only

Smaller objects like barricades, low walls, and barrels. They provide cover (extra saves when shot at) but do not make you Obscured â€” your attacker still gets all their hits, they just have a harder time getting through your saves.
